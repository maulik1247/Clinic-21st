Nursing Homes (ASNH:2011)

1.0 Procedure for Certification to Accreditation Standard for Nursing Homes (hereafter called as ASNH).

Intent

To define well-laid procedure for accreditation so that Nursing Home can upgrade and is well aware of steps required.

Interested Nursing Home (hereafter called as NH) will have to procure start-up kit which will include standard, draft manual & self-appraisal form from FEQH’s office by making payment of Rs.5,000/= favoring Association of Medical Consultants.

  1. The Nursing Home shall upgrade its facilities in order to meet the requirements of ASNH.

  1. NH shall approach FEQH with filled-in self appraisal form along with audit fees favoring FEQH as detailed in annexure 1.

  1. A FEQH will scrutinize application and if found acceptable, nominate audit team which will visit the NH on a mutually convenient date & time and conduct the audit.

  2. B If the application form is incomplete or has deficiencies, NH will be informed accordingly to enable them to resubmit the same after necessary corrections. fter receipt of acceptable form, steps mentioned in 1.4 A will be followed.


  1. The Nursing Home will be informed about the outcome of the audit within 30 days. The audit team will either


  1. Approve the Nursing Home for certification.

or

  1. Ask to make modifications to meet ASNH.

In case of 1.5.2, a follow-up visit may be required. In case of follow-up visit, re-visit fee of Rs. 5,000 will be charged.

  • Certified Nursing Homes will be informed about acceptance and will be conferred with the certificate. Their name will be put under certified members on the website www.qcimumbai.com.

Facility Standard for Nursing Homes.

Definition: Any commercial / charitable establishment registered under Section 5 of Maharashtra (Bombay) Nursing Home Registration Act or daycare center doing ambulatory surgeries.

Intent

To specify minimum infrastructure requirement and their objective elements.

2.1 Scope of the services:

  1. Name of the NH and its permanent location with relevant details, year of establishment shall be documented. Details of ownership of NH with legal identity shall be declared.

  2. Details of scope of services provided should be declared by NH Management. The services being provided shall be governed by competent regulatory, statutory, or legal requirement applicable from time to time.

2.2 Medical professionals:

  1. NH shall list all the Medical Professionals whose services are available in NH. The list shall include their names, degrees, Council registration number, and contact details.

2.3 Waiting rooms:

  1. Shall be well-lit, comfortable with recreation material to make waiting experience educative, informative and relaxing. Emergency light, clean toilet and safe drinking water should be available free of charge.

  2. Quality Policy shall be displayed

  3. Patients’ rights and responsibilities shall be displayed

Shall be well-lit, comfortable and have basic necessities like examination table, x-ray viewer, weighing scale, thermometer, BP apparatus, examination trolley, wash basin, glucometer, nebulizer etc. There should be facility to provide necessary privacy during examination.

Ward / Patients’ Rooms:

  1. Shall be provided with all essential items like cot with call bell, side table, wall clock, toilet, bathroom, stand for IV fluids, chair for attendant etc.

  2. Portable suction machine, ambu bag, plastic/ rubber sheet on cot shall be available.

  3. There shall be adequate number of urine pots and bed pans, emergency light and mosquito mats wherever necessary.

  4. There should be at least 1 toilet per 5 cots.

  5. In case of general wards, the toilet should have door closure and exhaust fan.

  6. Patients’ linen shall be changed daily or earlier, if soiled.

3.0 Staff

Intent

To groom staff as good healthcare workers in safe environment. To encourage human resources to orient with good practices in infection control, sterilization & patient’s care.

3.1 Staff - other than Nursing & Technical:


  1. Biodata of all employees shall be maintained at NH.

  2. Shall be provided with lab coat/uniform and identity card.

  3. Staff shall be aware about the accreditation program.

  4. Staff coming in contact with patients shall undergo health check-up for communicable diseases once in three years.

  5. Staff should undergo training for bio-medical waste disposal.

  6. Staff should undergo induction program before assuming duties.

Item Minimum Optimum Excellent

3.2 Staff - Nursing & Technical Staff:

  1. Same as above and Xerox copies of certificates should be available.

  2. Nurses will be provided with appropriate uniform and shall be aware of NH’s medicine scoring system.

  3. Should be aware of various procedures that are practiced in NH.

  4. Staff should undergo training for first aid.

  5. Nursing Audit / skill evaluation should be conducted at least once during validity of the ASNH.

Infection control:

  1. Waste segregation, hand wash procedures and other preventive practices are well practiced.

  1. All nursing & technical staff members shall be immunized against communicable diseases.

  2. Systematic efforts should be made to control hospital acquired infection.

4.0 Facility management and safety :

Intent

Provision of safe and secure environment for patients, their families, staff and other visitors.

4.1 Risk Mitigation:

  1. The Nursing Home shall have adequate number of fire extinguishers (1.5 Kgs per 1500 Sq.Ft.) & first aid box. Safety issue should be looked into on a case to case basis.

  2. List of emergency numbers shall be available with key staff members present in NH.


4.2 Social accountability:

  1. Child labor shall not be employed.

  2. Staff members shall not be exploited or physically punished.

  3. There should be staff welfare program.

  4. There should be program for under privileged of society.

5.0 Suggestion / Complaint box:

Intent

Suggestions and complaints are learning tools for any organization and need to be looked into seriously. Suggestions and complaints from other stake holders also need to be obtained.

5.1 Patients grievances:

  1. A Suggestion/Complaint Box shall be available at convenient locations for patients/customers to lodge their complaints or submit suggestions.

  2. There should be a mechanism to redress all complaints/ suggestions.

  3. Nursing Home should have the facility of obtaining suggestions / complaints from all the stake holders.

Item Minimum Optimum Excellent
Objective element 1 1, 2 1, 2, 3

6.0 Equipments:

Intent

The intent of the standard is to ensure that all the equipments are in well maintained condition and are dependable.

6.1 Equipments – List, maintenance & calibration status

  1. NH shall have a list of important equipments. All equipments should be clean, stain-free and in well maintained condition.

  2. Equipments, which are not working, should be suitably identified.

  3. NH shall maintain list of equipments that need calibration.

  4. All the equipments should have history card/ logbook / register with service / repair record.

  5. Manuals & software CDs, if any, pertaining to equipments should be readily available for reference.

Item Minimum Optimum Excellent
Objective element 1 1, 2 1, 2, 3, 4, 5

7.0 Continual improvement:

Intent

To incorporate and document the history and pathway to take the Nursing Home to newer heights.

7.1 NH should have objectives to improve its services.

  1. The intent of the standard is to encourage NH to innovate and improve continually. Documented evidence of improvements made from previous audit should be available.

8) Additional requirements

The NH shall fulfill below mentioned additional requirements:

  1. NH shall have facility to maintain cold chain.

  2. Emergency medicines should also be available at a place other than OT.

  3. List of procedures that need consent form should be available. The consent form should be suitable for the purpose.

  4. Criteria to be available with staff to identify patients who may require immediate attention.

  5. Staff and doctors should know the procedure to handle medico legal cases.

  6. NH shall maintain master file containing all stationery, formats, forms, broucheres etc. used in NH.

  7. NH shall have 3 hours power back up.

  8. IPD records and medicines prescribed shall be legible and wherever required, signed, dated and timed.

  9. Informed consent should be obtained before photographing a patient which will reveal his / her identity.

  10. Wherever video cameras are installed, suitable warning shall be prominently displayed.

  11. The key paramedic staff shall be trained at least for one full day in a year.

  12. Staff shall be aware about patient’s rights and responsibilities.

  13. Evacuation plan shall be ready for disaster management.

Item Minimum Optimum Excellent
Objective element 1 to 2 1 to 9 1 to 13

Note:

  • Activities not documented will not be considered as done.
  • Shall means compulsory and should means recommended.
  • OT size for ophthalmic set up can be relaxed upto 20 sq.ft. for Optimum & Excellent grade.
  • For orthopedic set-up doing joint replacements, Heppa filter and laminar air flow is must for “Excellent grade”.
